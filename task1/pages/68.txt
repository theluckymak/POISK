<p><b>Guido van Rossum</b> (<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1177148991">.mw-parser-output .IPA-label-small{font-size:85%}.mw-parser-output .references .IPA-label-small,.mw-parser-output .infobox .IPA-label-small,.mw-parser-output .navbox .IPA-label-small{font-size:100%}</style><span class="IPA-label IPA-label-small">Dutch:</span> <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="nl-Latn-fonipa"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Dutch" title="Help:IPA/Dutch">&#91;ˈxidoː<span class="wrap"> </span>vɑn<span class="wrap"> </span>ˈrɔsʏm&#93;</a></span>; born 31 January 1956) is a Dutch <a href="/wiki/Programmer" title="Programmer">programmer</a>. He is the creator of the <a href="/wiki/Python_(programming_language)" title="Python (programming language)">Python programming language</a>, for which he was the "<a href="/wiki/Benevolent_dictator_for_life" title="Benevolent dictator for life">benevolent dictator for life</a>" (BDFL) until he stepped down from the position on 12 July 2018.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> He remained a member of the Python Steering Council through 2019, and withdrew from nominations for the 2020 election.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p>

<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Life_and_education">Life and education</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Guido_van_Rossum&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life and education"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>Van Rossum was born and raised in the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>, where he received a master's degree in mathematics and <a href="/wiki/Computer_science" title="Computer science">computer science</a> from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Amsterdam" title="University of Amsterdam">University of Amsterdam</a> in 1982. He received a bronze medal in 1974 in the <a href="/wiki/International_Mathematical_Olympiad" title="International Mathematical Olympiad">International Mathematical Olympiad</a>.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> His brother, <a href="/wiki/Just_van_Rossum" title="Just van Rossum">Just van Rossum</a>, is a <a href="/wiki/Type_designer" class="mw-redirect" title="Type designer">type designer</a> and programmer who designed the <a href="/wiki/Typeface" title="Typeface">typeface</a> used in the "Python Powered" logo.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>Van Rossum lives in <a href="/wiki/Belmont,_California" title="Belmont, California">Belmont</a>, California, with his wife, Kim Knapp,<sup id="cite&#95;ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and their son.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Work">Work</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Guido_van_Rossum&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Work"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Centrum_Wiskunde_and_Informatica"><i>Centrum Wiskunde and Informatica</i></h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Guido_van_Rossum&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Centrum Wiskunde and Informatica"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>While working at the <i><a href="/wiki/Centrum_Wiskunde_%26_Informatica" title="Centrum Wiskunde &amp; Informatica">Centrum Wiskunde &amp; Informatica</a></i> (CWI), Van Rossum wrote and contributed a <a href="/wiki/Glob_(programming)" title="Glob (programming)">glob()</a> routine to <a href="/wiki/Berkeley_Software_Distribution" title="Berkeley Software Distribution">BSD</a> <a href="/wiki/Unix" title="Unix">Unix</a> in 1986<sup id="cite&#95;ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>14<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and helped develop the <a href="/wiki/ABC_(programming_language)" title="ABC (programming language)">ABC programming language</a>. He once stated, "I try to mention ABC's influence because I'm indebted to everything I learned during that project and to the people who worked on it."<sup id="cite&#95;ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>15<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> He also created <a href="/wiki/Grail_(web_browser)" title="Grail (web browser)">Grail</a>, an early <a href="/wiki/Web_browser" title="Web browser">web browser</a> written in <a href="/wiki/Python_(programming_language)" title="Python (programming language)">Python</a>, and engaged in discussions about the <a href="/wiki/HTML" title="HTML">HTML</a> standard.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>16<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>He has worked for various research institutes, including the CWI in the Netherlands, the U.S. <a href="/wiki/National_Institute_of_Standards_and_Technology" title="National Institute of Standards and Technology">National Institute of Standards and Technology</a> (NIST), and the <a href="/wiki/Corporation_for_National_Research_Initiatives" title="Corporation for National Research Initiatives">Corporation for National Research Initiatives</a> (CNRI). In May 2000, he left CNRI along with three other Python core developers to work for tech startup BeOpen.com, which subsequently collapsed by October of the same year.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>17<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>18<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> From late 2000 until 2003 he worked for <a href="/wiki/Zope" title="Zope">Zope</a> Corporation. In 2003 Van Rossum left Zope for Elemental Security. While there he worked on a custom programming language for the organization.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>19<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Google">Google</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Guido_van_Rossum&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Google"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>From 2005 to December 2012, Van Rossum worked at Google, where he spent half of his time developing the Python language. 
At Google, he developed Mondrian, a web-based <a href="/wiki/Code_review" title="Code review">code review</a> system written in Python and used within the company. He named the software after the Dutch painter <a href="/wiki/Piet_Mondrian" title="Piet Mondrian">Piet Mondrian</a>.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>20<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> He named <a href="/wiki/Rietveld_(software)" title="Rietveld (software)">Rietveld</a>, another related software project, after <a href="/wiki/Gerrit_Rietveld" title="Gerrit Rietveld">Gerrit Rietveld</a>, a Dutch designer.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>21<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> On 7 December 2012, Van Rossum left Google.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-twitter277126763295944705&#95;22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-twitter277126763295944705-22"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>22<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Dropbox">Dropbox</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Guido_van_Rossum&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Dropbox"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>In January 2013, Van Rossum started working at the <a href="/wiki/Cloud_storage" title="Cloud storage">cloud file storage</a> company <a href="/wiki/Dropbox_(service)" class="mw-redirect" title="Dropbox (service)">Dropbox</a>.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>23<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>24<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>In October 2019, Van Rossum left Dropbox and officially retired.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>25<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>26<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>27<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Microsoft">Microsoft</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Guido_van_Rossum&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Microsoft"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>On 12 November 2020 Van Rossum announced that he was coming out of retirement to join the Developer Division at <a href="/wiki/Microsoft" title="Microsoft">Microsoft</a>. He currently holds the title Distinguished Engineer at Microsoft.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>28<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>29<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>30<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Python">Python</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Guido_van_Rossum&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Python"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1320445320" /><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Python_(programming_language)" title="Python (programming language)">Python (programming language)</a></div>
<p>In December 1989, Van Rossum had been looking for a <span style="padding-right:.15em;">"</span>'hobby' programming project that would keep [him] occupied during the week around Christmas" as his office was closed when he decided to write an <a href="/wiki/Interpreter_(computing)" title="Interpreter (computing)">interpreter</a> for a "new scripting language [he] had been thinking about lately: a descendant of <a href="/wiki/ABC_(programming_language)" title="ABC (programming language)">ABC</a> that would appeal to <a href="/wiki/Unix" title="Unix">Unix</a>/<a href="/wiki/C_(programming_language)" title="C (programming language)">C</a> hackers". He chose the name Python for his programming language not because of the snake type but the comedy series <i><a href="/wiki/Monty_Python%27s_Flying_Circus" title="Monty Python&#39;s Flying Circus">Monty Python's Flying Circus</a></i>.
</p><p>He has explained that Python's predecessor, <a href="/wiki/ABC_(programming_language)" title="ABC (programming language)">ABC</a>, was inspired by <a href="/wiki/SETL" title="SETL">SETL</a>, noting that ABC co-developer <a href="/wiki/Lambert_Meertens" title="Lambert Meertens">Lambert Meertens</a> had "spent a year with the SETL group at <a href="/wiki/New_York_University" title="New York University">NYU</a> before coming up with the final ABC design".<sup id="cite&#95;ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>31<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>On 12 July 2018, Van Rossum announced that he would be stepping down from the position of <a href="/wiki/Benevolent_dictator_for_life" title="Benevolent dictator for life">benevolent dictator for life</a> of the Python programming language.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>32<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="&quot;Computer_Programming_for_Everybody&quot;_proposal"><span id=".22Computer_Programming_for_Everybody.22_proposal"></span>"Computer Programming for Everybody" proposal</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Guido_van_Rossum&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: &quot;Computer Programming for Everybody&quot; proposal"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>In 1999, Van Rossum submitted a funding proposal to the <a href="/wiki/Defense_Advanced_Research_Projects_Agency" class="mw-redirect" title="Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency">Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency</a> (DARPA) called "Computer Programming for Everybody", in which he further defined his goals for Python:
</p>
<ul><li>An easy and intuitive language just as powerful as major competitors</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Open-source_software" title="Open-source software">Open source</a>, so anyone can contribute to its development</li>
<li>Code that is as understandable as plain English</li>
<li>Suitability for everyday tasks, allowing for short development times</li></ul>
<p>In 2019, Python became the second most popular language on <a href="/wiki/GitHub" title="GitHub">GitHub</a>, the largest <a href="/wiki/Source_code" title="Source code">source code</a> management website on the internet, after <a href="/wiki/JavaScript" title="JavaScript">JavaScript</a>.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>33<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In 2024 Python became the most used language on <a href="/wiki/GitHub" title="GitHub">GitHub</a>, overtaking <a href="/wiki/JavaScript" title="JavaScript">JavaScript</a> after a 10-year run as the most used language.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>34<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> According to a programming language popularity survey<sup id="cite&#95;ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>35<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> it is consistently among the top 10 most mentioned languages in job postings. Furthermore, Python has been among the 10 most popular programming languages every year since 2004 according to the <a href="/wiki/TIOBE_Programming_Community_Index" class="mw-redirect" title="TIOBE Programming Community Index">TIOBE Programming Community Index</a> and got the number one spot on the index in October 2021.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>36<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Awards">Awards</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Guido_van_Rossum&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Awards"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<ul><li>At the 2002 <a href="/wiki/FOSDEM" title="FOSDEM">FOSDEM</a> conference in <a href="/wiki/Brussels" title="Brussels">Brussels</a>, Van Rossum received the 2001 <a href="/wiki/Award_for_the_Advancement_of_Free_Software" class="mw-redirect" title="Award for the Advancement of Free Software">Award for the Advancement of Free Software</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Free_Software_Foundation" title="Free Software Foundation">Free Software Foundation</a> (FSF) for his work on Python.</li>
<li>In May 2003, he received a <a href="/wiki/NLUUG" title="NLUUG">NLUUG</a> Award.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>37<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup></li>
<li>In 2006, he was recognized as an <a href="/wiki/List_of_distinguished_members_of_the_Association_for_Computing_Machinery" title="List of distinguished members of the Association for Computing Machinery">ACM Distinguished Engineer</a>.</li>
<li>In 2018, he was made a Fellow of the <a href="/wiki/Computer_History_museum" class="mw-redirect" title="Computer History museum">Computer History museum</a>.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>38<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup></li>
<li>In 2019, he was awarded the honorary title of Dijkstra Fellow by <a href="/wiki/Centrum_Wiskunde_%26_Informatica" title="Centrum Wiskunde &amp; Informatica">CWI</a>.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>39<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup></li>
<li>In 2023, he was awarded the <a href="/wiki/C%26C_Prize" title="C&amp;C Prize">C&amp;C Prize</a> by NEC Corporation for developing the Python programming language.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>40<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup></li></ul>
